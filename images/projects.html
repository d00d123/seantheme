Intranet Training Sessions

Eight months before launch it was time to train all of my department webmasters. I ran training sessions weekly throughout July of 2015 and covered basic page editing, how to use the style guide and how to approach file hierarchy. Sessions were two hours plus time for questions. I also encouraged users to attend multiple sessions until they felt comfortable.

I continue to support and provide training to any staff that require it. Most recently I ran a tips and tricks session during a professional development day to help office staff learn how to use the intranet and leverage all of its features.

Training Manuals

I always try to be available to my users to provide support whenever they need it. Regardless of whether a manual is available, I understand that a detailed, personal explanation can help many users feel more comfortable. However, for times when I'm unavailable, it's important there are detailed training manuals in plain language. I make sure to approach each manual as if it's my first time using the product. No detail is too small to mention.

Pupil Accommodation Review Webpage

A review is currently underway to examine a proposal to close Gordon Graydon Memorial School. This Pupil Accommodation Review (PAR) is a critical communications piece and the webpage for it on the main board website is the primary communications method for PAR information. The information is dense and complicated. Working on the page was a great opportunity to work on my user experience skills as well as work on my ability to collaborate with groups that have different communications goals and priorities.

Short Link

In my first year with the board I got the idea to buy a low cost domain and brand all of our shortlinks on social media using bit.ly. We purchased pdsb.me and then got the shortlink setup six months later (the delay was due to labour disruptions). Now news releases and other communications items are linked with a pdsb.me link on our social media!

Special Education Plan

The old Special Education Plan page is still in use because this is a project in progress. I'm helping to refine and simplify the way the Special Education Plan is presented. Working with the Superintendent of Special Education, I've used text formatting and some CSS to make headings clearer in the plan.

The page is still huge with a lot of text but as the project moves forward, images and other graphics will be added to help break up content. Notice how when you jump to a section it is highlighted briefly, to bring the user's attention to their target. Remember: this page is in beta.

Student Volunteer Page

This was a little side project I worked on with my contact in our IT department at Peel. The older volunteer board was just a big huge table with the organization name and a PDF that they would submit. There was no expiry tracking and no extra information.

I developed a layout that included collapsable items, position summaries, and expiry dates on the back end so that old postings disappear when they are no longer valid. A subscription option is being worked on now so that students can get weekly emails with all the postings.
